About Me

I'm a Marine Corps Veteran with a passion for video games. I'm currently studying computer with a minor in game design and development at Quinnipiac University. At Quinnipiac, I've learned various programming langauges (Scala, Java, JavaScript, C#, and Python), object-oriented programming model, computer architecture, operating systems, data structures, algorithm design, and project management. Video game development is how I apply what I learned in my computer sciences classes which helps me make robust and mechanically sound video games.

Why I Chose Computer Science

  • The sense of accomplishment from figuring out a problem. In the Marine Corps, I was an artillery mechanic. Half of my job revolved around maintaining the weapon and the other half entailed figuring out what was wrong with it if something wasn't working. I enjoyed the process of diagnosing the issue, fixing it, and making it work again. I get the same enjoyment from programming. I'm given a problem, and it's my job to find an efficient solution and implement it.
  • I'm able to make video games instead of just playing them. Although I acknowledge that other parts of video game development are important, such as art and music, I believe that making a game that's mechanically sound is the most important aspect. Nothing matters if the game doesn't work.
  • I've heard this saying somewhere: "Major in what will get you money, minor in what you love."
